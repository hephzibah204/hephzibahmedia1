## Problems & Pain Points
**Core problem:** Nigerian businesses have "informational" websites that don't convert, and they struggle with disconnected marketing and print vendors.
**Why alternatives fall short:**
- Generic agencies don't understand "WhatsApp culture" or Nigerian payment friction.
- Freelancers often lack the end-to-end capability (Strategy -> Design -> Print).
- Low-cost printers sacrifice quality and brand consistency.
**What it costs them:** Lost leads, wasted ad spend, high bounce rates, and a "generic" brand image that fails to build trust.
**Emotional tension:** Fear of being left behind in the digital economy; frustration with technical complexity.

## Competitive Landscape
**Direct:** Local digital agencies in Lagos/Ogun — fall short because they often lack the physical print capability or 12+ years of deep market fluency.
**Secondary:** DIY Website Builders (Wix/Shopify) — fall short because they don't provide the localized strategy or persuasive Nigerian-specific copywriting.
**Indirect:** Personal Assistants or in-house juniors — fall short because they lack specialized expertise in growth hacking and technical SEO.
