      # Local SEO for Nigerian Businesses: Dominate Google Maps & Business Profiles

      By Hephzibah Media Team · Published December 22, 2024
      Local SEO wins the 'near me' searches. When someone searches 'web design Lagos' or 'printing services Abuja,' Google shows map results + local businesses. Here's exactly how to rank at the top of those results.
    
  

  
    
      ## Claim & Optimize Your Google Business Profile

      
1. Go to google.com/business, search your business name. 2. If it exists, claim it. If not, create it. 3. Complete 100% of the profile: all photos, description, hours, services, website. 4. Add 1015 photos (office, team, past work, before-afters). 5. Verify via postcard (23 weeks) or phone (instant). Verified profiles rank 23× higher.

      ## NAP Consistency Across the Web

      
NAP = Name, Address, Phone. Every mention of your business must be identical: same spelling, same format. Create citations on: Google Business, Bing Places, Nigerian directories (VConnect, BusinessList.com.ng, Jumia Services). Update all of them if you move. Inconsistent NAP = lower local rankings.

      ## Location Pages with Unique Content

      
If you serve multiple cities (Lagos, Abuja, Ikeja), create a unique page for each. Not just swapping the city name  genuinely different content: local testimonials, local case studies, community involvement. Example: 'Web Design in Lagos' page mentions Lekki-specific clients; 'Web Design in Abuja' page mentions GRA Abuja clients.

      ## Build Local Backlinks

      
Get mentioned on Nigerian media sites, blogs, and business networks. Write a guest post for a Lagos business blog. Sponsor a local event (gets mentioned on their website). Partner with complementary businesses who link to you. Local links signal 'this business serves this community.'

      ## Reviews: The Local SEO Multiplier

      
Google prioritises businesses with recent 5-star reviews. Aim for 40+ reviews (threshold for competitive rankings). After every project, email customers: 'We'd love your feedback on Google [link].' Respond to every review (positive and negative). Reviews also influence CTR  4.5? businesses get 25% more clicks than 3.5?.

      ## Question & Answer Management

      
Go to your Google Business Profile, add FAQs. Customers see these before they call or visit. Example: 'How much does web design cost?' 'Do you offer payment plans?' Answer in 23 sentences, clearly. This also helps Google understand your business better.

      ## Local Schema Markup

      
Add LocalBusiness schema to your website (already done in these templates). Include: address, phone, hours, service areas. If you serve 10 cities, add ServiceArea schema listing all of them. This helps Google understand your service radius.
